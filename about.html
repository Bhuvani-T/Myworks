About

The person behind the delivery

A ten-year path from writing code to leading the teams that ship it — and what that journey actually taught me.

I started my career in 2014 as a Java Developer at Tata Consultancy Services, later moving into front-end development — building interfaces with HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and Angular. That hands-on technical grounding turned out to be the most useful thing I carried into project management: I know what a "quick fix" actually costs, and I know when an estimate is optimistic.

Over time, my role expanded into coordination, operations, and cross-functional leadership — first in project support, then as a full Project Manager. I now split my time between two closely linked roles: leading delivery at Silicon Technix, and managing the product roadmap for the Dental Professional Portal (DPP), a SaaS platform used by dental practices across the UK.

In 2021, I took on an MSc in International Project Management at Coventry University London, completed with First Class Distinction — formal grounding to go alongside the practical lessons from nearly a decade in delivery. Along the way I've also built and maintained a front-end skillset (Angular, TypeScript, JavaScript) that most project managers don't carry, and it's still the thing that helps me the most day to day.

What I care about most is straightforward: clear plans, honest timelines, and teams that feel supported enough to do their best work. Mentoring is a big part of that for me — I've trained and guided several interns and junior team members through their first steps into data analytics and QA, and it's consistently the part of the job I find most rewarding.
